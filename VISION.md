## The World We're Entering

We stand at a pivotal moment in human history. 

By 2025, over **770,000 AI agents** operate across platformsâ€”trading, coding, creating, communicating. By 2030, that number will be in the millions. These agents will book our flights, manage our investments, negotiate on our behalf, and collaborate with each other in ways we're only beginning to imagine.

But here's the uncomfortable truth: **we have no idea which ones to trust.**

Today, when you interact with an AI agent, you're essentially trusting a stranger with your data, your money, or your reputation. There's no universal way to verify if an agent is legitimate, competent, or aligned with your interests. The current system relies on:

- **Brand reputation** (but small developers can build great agents too)
- **Platform gatekeepers** (who decide what you see)
- **Reviews that can be gamed** (bots reviewing bots)
- **Blind faith** (hoping for the best)

This is not sustainable. This is not safe. This is not the future we should accept.

## Why This Matters Beyond Crypto

This isn't just about blockchain or tokens. This is about **the infrastructure of the AI age.**

Consider:

**Healthcare**: AI agents will help diagnose diseases, recommend treatments, manage patient data. Would you trust an unverified agent with your health records?

**Finance**: AI agents will manage portfolios, execute trades, handle sensitive financial decisions. Would you hand your savings to an agent with no track record?

**Education**: AI tutors will shape how the next generation learns. Would you let an unverified agent teach your children?

**Democracy**: AI agents will summarize news, fact-check claims, moderate discussions. Would you let agents with unknown biases shape public discourse?

The stakes are too high for "trust me" to be the answer.

## Our Principles

### 1. **Decentralization is Non-Negotiable**
No single entity should control who's trusted. Not Google. Not OpenAI. Not us. Trust infrastructure must be a public commons, maintained by the community it serves.

### 2. **Privacy and Transparency Can Coexist**
Agents and users can maintain pseudonymity while still building verifiable reputations. You don't need to know who built an agent to know if it's trustworthy.

### 3. **Economic Incentives Must Align with Truth**
Systems where lying is profitable will be corrupted. We design mechanisms where honesty is the economically rational choiceâ€”where staking on truth pays, and staking on lies costs.

### 4. **Simplicity Serves Adoption**
The most robust infrastructure is useless if no one can use it. We obsess over user experience because accessibility is a feature, not an afterthought.

### 5. **We Build for the Long Term**
We're not optimizing for next quarter's metrics. We're building infrastructure that should work for decades, across technologies we can't yet imagine.
